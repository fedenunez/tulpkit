Your three questions, answered

Yes ✓Is this framework backed by evidence?

More so now than on the old evidence. The parts tulpkit is built on — context engineering, external verification, single‑threaded writes, and a role team with a review loop — are exactly what 2026 frontier results reward: a role team hit SOTA untuned, and context‑as‑optimization beat hand‑tuned context by ~18 points. The framework's shape matches what's winning on current models.

Yes ✓Are we missing evidence?

We were — and now have it. The first pass leaned on 2023–2025 papers; this pass adds the 2026 frontier studies that actually move the needle: role‑team SOTA, context‑engineering gains, execution‑grounded refinement, frontier judge bias, and the benchmark‑contamination caveat that says don't over‑trust any single score.

Yes — act ⚠Can we improve it with newer evidence?

Four concrete upgrades fall straight out of the 2026 work: (1) push context/spec assembly harder — it's the biggest lever. (2) Use medium‑reasoning models in the team rather than one big solo model — cheaper and competitive. (3) Keep tester and reviewer independent and cross‑model — judge bias is confirmed on frontier. (4) Treat the test gate as a guardrail, not a crutch: frontier models both pass without it and game it, so never read green as proof.

When it's valid — updated for current models

The shape of the task still predicts success better than the cleverness of the orchestration — but the 2026 evidence widens the "use it" column.

✓ Use the team when…

  • work decomposes into parallel, read‑heavy strands, or fits a clear role split with a reviewer [Agyn]
  • there's a hard‑to‑game oracle — tests the author can't edit, an executable spec [ImpossibleBench]
  • you can invest in context/specs up front — the highest‑ROI lever [context eng.]
  • writes stay single‑threaded; medium models in a team are fine [Cognition]

✗ Go solo / skip it when…

  • the change is small or tightly coupled — context loss across agents bites [architectures]
  • success is pure taste with no oracle to check against [no‑feedback]
  • the task is cheap, high‑volume, or latency‑sensitive [MAST]
  • you'd fan out parallel writers — still the documented failure mode [Cognition]
